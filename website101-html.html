With Duke Away in Washington, Duchess Charms Harlem Crowds

By MATT FLEGENHEIMERDEC. 8, 2014

The guests peered into Classroom 316, where 3- and 4-year-olds from a Harlem children’s center had gathered around a table to decorate picture frames with stickers.

“Do you know who’s coming to visit today?” an administrator asked the group.

“Princess!” a girl named April shouted.

And then, the Duchess of Cambridge sidled up beside her, in a diminutive seat labeled “chair,” and reached for the stickers.

“Are these your friends?” the duchess asked the boy to her left, Sammy, who wore a Mickey Mouse shirt.

He began to name the entire class, before returning to the visitor. She smiled.

For about 90 minutes, she was the Duchess of East 110th Street.

With her husband, Prince William, spending much of the day in Washington — where he met President Obama and spoke at the World Bank — Catherine, the former Kate Middleton, spent the first leg of her first morning in New York at Northside Center for Child Development, wrapping gifts, nodding politely and discussing the merits of a recent cinematic princess with students.
